Applying the Governance Framework to L&D activities

Select the scenario and explore how this AI tool aligns with the framework.

Scenario 1: Mastering Compliance

L&D Challenge: Compliance training (e.g., Anti-Money Laundering/AML, Code of Conduct) is mandatory but often forgotten. Staff struggle to apply the formal rules to real-world, nuanced customer situations, creating potential risks.

Formal Learning

A new teller has just completed their annual AML e-learning module. To solidify their understanding, they ask the AI:

"Summarise the top 5 red flags for a potential structured transaction according to our bank's AML policy."

Experiential Learning

A week later, that same teller faces a tricky situation. Instead of escalating or making a mistake, they discreetly use the AI:

"A customer is making a $9,800 cash deposit and seems nervous. What are the immediate steps I should take according to policy? Provide me with some appropriate questions to ask them."

Scenario 2: Accelerating Product Readiness

L&D Challenge: When the bank launches a new, complex product (e.g., a "First Home Buyer Eco Loan"), the L&D team must train hundreds of lending specialists quickly. A one-off webinar and a PDF guide are often not enough for specialists to feel confident.

Experiential Learning

A lending specialist wants to practice handling customer objections before a client meeting. They use the AI to simulate a conversation:

"Act as a potential first home buyer who is skeptical about the benefits of an 'Eco Loan'. I will explain the product to you. Your goal is to ask me three difficult questions."

Social Learning

After a successful meeting, the specialist asks the AI to help them share their winning technique with their peers:

"I just successfully pitched the new Eco Loan by comparing its long-term energy savings to the slightly higher interest rate. Turn this concept into a clear, 3-point summary for our team's SharePoint page."

Scenario 3: Developing New Leaders

L&D Challenge: A high-performing employee is promoted to Branch Manager. They have strong technical skills but lack experience in handling difficult leadership situations, such as addressing underperformance.

Formal Learning

The new manager has taken a course on giving constructive feedback. To refresh their memory, they ask the AI:

"Explain the STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) model for giving behavioural feedback. Give me an example relevant to a bank teller who has been making minor cash-handling errors."

Experiential Learning

The manager now needs to prepare for the actual conversation. They use the AI as a private sounding board and coach:

"I need to have a feedback conversation with an employee about their negative tone with customers. This is our first formal discussion about it. Help me structure the opening of the conversation to be supportive but clear. Provide three different opening statements."
