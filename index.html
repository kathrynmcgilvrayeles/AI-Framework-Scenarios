Applying the Governance Framework to L&D activities

Scenario 1: Mastering Compliance in the Flow of Work

L&D Challenge: Compliance training (e.g., Anti-Money Laundering/AML, Code of Conduct) is mandatory but often forgotten. Staff struggle to apply the formal rules to real-world, nuanced customer situations, creating potential risks.

Formal Learning

A new teller has just completed their annual AML e-learning module. To solidify their understanding, they ask the AI:

"Summarise the top 5 red flags for a potential structured transaction according to our bank's AML policy."

Experiential Learning

A week later, that same teller is faced with a tricky situation. Instead of escalating immediately or making a mistake, they discreetly use the AI:

"A customer is making a $9,800 cash deposit and seems nervous. What are the immediate steps I should take according to policy? Provide me with some appropriate questions to ask them without escalating the situation unnecessarily."

Discussion Question

Discussion Question: How does providing immediate, in-the-moment performance support like this change our approach to risk management and reduce the pressure on team leaders?

Scenario 2: Accelerating Product Knowledge and Sales Readiness

L&D Challenge: When the bank launches a new, complex product (e.g., a "First Home Buyer Eco Loan"), the L&D team must train hundreds of lending specialists quickly. A one-off webinar and a PDF guide are often not enough for specialists to feel confident.

Experiential Learning

A lending specialist wants to practice handling customer objections before a client meeting. They use the AI to simulate a conversation:

"Act as a potential first home buyer who is skeptical about the benefits of an 'Eco Loan'. I will explain the product to you. Your goal is to ask me three difficult questions, focusing on interest rates and long-term costs."

Social Learning

The specialist has a successful client meeting and identifies a brilliant way to explain the loan's benefits. To share this with their team, they ask the AI:

"I just successfully pitched the new Eco Loan by comparing its long-term energy savings to the slightly higher interest rate. Turn this concept into a clear, 3-point summary that I can post on our team's SharePoint page to help my colleagues."

Discussion Question

Discussion Question: Beyond role-playing, in what other ways could this AI 'Partner' be used to foster a culture of continuous, shared learning within sales teams?

Scenario 3: Developing New People Leaders

L&D Challenge: A high-performing employee has been promoted to Branch Manager. They have strong technical skills but lack experience in handling difficult leadership situations, such as addressing underperformance.

Formal Learning

The new manager has taken a course on giving constructive feedback. To refresh their memory on a specific model, they ask the AI:

"Explain the STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) model for giving behavioural feedback. Give me an example relevant to a bank teller who has been making minor cash-handling errors."

Experiential & Social Learning

The manager now needs to prepare for the actual conversation. This is the most challenging part. They use the AI as a private sounding board and coach:

"I need to have a feedback conversation with an employee about their negative tone with customers. This is our first formal discussion about it. Help me structure the opening of the conversation to be supportive but clear. Provide three different opening statements I could use."
